Sometimes everything will die off! This is normal: this is a simulation modeling nature. Just refresh the page.

Big yellow circles represent planets, small rectangles represent plants and small triangle represent herbivores.
The energy of plants and herbivores is displayed next to them.
Each turn plants and herbivores will lose some energy.
For plants this can be offset by photosyntesizing the planet's energy.
The number in the middle of each planet is how much energy is available to each plant occupying the planet. The energy available is inversely proportional to the number of plants occupying the planet. The intuition is that plants can crowd each other and reduce the energy available to each of them.
Herbivores can feed off of nearby plants, transfering some of the energy from the plant to themselves. If no plants are available nearby, the herbivore may jump to a new spot on the planet
Both plants and herbivores reproduce asexually whenever they reach certain energy. The offspring start off with less than 1/2 of the parent's energy.
Both plants and herbivores have a small chance to begin a migration to another planet if they have at least 50 energy (migration costs 40). Plants will only migrate to planets with a higher energy number and herbivores will only migrate to planets with better plants-to-herbivores ratio.
